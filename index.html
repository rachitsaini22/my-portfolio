How to use this web site

1st step :- Download and Install the App: Visit your device's respective app store (e.g., Apple App Store for iOS, Google Play Store for Android). Search for the FLORA-TECH plant disease detector app. Download and install the app.


2nd step :- Grant Necessary Permissions: Allow the app access to your device's camera and, if needed, location services. This enables the app to function properly.


3rd step :- Open the App: Launch the app by tapping its icon.


4th step :- Identify and Detect Plant Diseases: Use the app's camera feature to identify and detect plant diseases. Point your device's camera at the plant or the affected area. Ensure good lighting conditions for accurate detection. Capture a clear picture of the plant or the affected region according to the app's guidelines, which may include centering the plant in the frame and capturing all relevant details.


5th step :- Analysis and Results: After capturing the image, the app will analyze it and provide results. Results may include the name of the disease and recommendations for treating the plant, which could involve using specific plant curing products.


6th step :- Save and Share Results (Optional): You can save the results for future reference or share them with gardening experts or agricultural extension services for more detailed advice.


7th step :- Take Action: Based on the app's recommendations, implement appropriate actions to manage or treat the plant disease. This might entail using specific pesticides, adjusting watering practices, pruning affected areas, or other measures. This guide should help users effectively utilize the FLORA-TECH app for plant disease detection and management.


Popular Diseases

Black Spot


In English


Black spot is a fungal disease, primarily affecting roses. It causes small, round, black or brown spots on leaves, leading to yellowing and premature leaf drop. Spread through spores in humid conditions, it's managed by pruning infected leaves, maintaining cleanliness, using fungicides, planting resistant varieties, and ensuring good air circulation around plants. Prevention is key to control

Septoria leaf


In English


Septoria leaf spot is a common fungal disease affecting various plants, creating circular gray-tan spots with dark margins on leaves. Favoring moist conditions, it spreads via spores carried by wind or water. Control involves crop rotation, sanitation, and, in severe cases, fungicides. Resistant plant varieties and preventive measures like proper spacing and reduced humidity help manage Septoria leaf spot effectively.

Mosaic viruses


In English


Mosaic viruses are plant diseases transmitted by insects. They cause a mosaic-like pattern of light and dark green or yellow-green on leaves, affecting various plants. Symptoms include leaf distortion, stunted growth, and reduced yield. Control measures involve planting resistant varieties, managing insect vectors, practicing sanitation, crop rotation, and regular testing to limit their spread and minimize damage to crops.

Bactrial spot


In English


Bacterial spot disease harms crops like tomatoes and peppers, causing small, water-soaked lesions that turn brown. It spreads through rain and contaminated tools. Controlling it is vital for crop health and yield, using methods like resistant plant varieties and copper sprays.

Late blight


In English


Late blight, caused by Phytophthora infestans, is a destructive plant disease affecting potatoes and tomatoes. It manifests as dark spots on leaves, stem lesions, and tuber rot, rapidly spreading in cool, humid conditions. Spores spread through wind and rain. Control measures include fungicides, crop rotation, and sanitation. Its historical impact, like the Irish Potato Famine, underscores its economic significance in agriculture.

Black spot


In English


Early blight, caused by the fungus Alternaria solani, is a common plant disease impacting crops like tomatoes and potatoes. It's marked by circular lesions with dark concentric rings on lower leaves, leading to yellowing and reduced yields. Spread through fungal spores via rain and wind, it thrives in warm, humid conditions. Control involves fungicides, cultural practices like crop rotation, and using resistant plant varieties to mitigate economic losses in agriculture.
